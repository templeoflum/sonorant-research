## Company Overview

Funktion-One was founded in 1992 by Tony Andrews and John Newsham, two veteran audio engineers who previously established and led Turbosound through the late 1970s-early 1990s. They are credited with developing several generations of revolutionary loudspeaker designs including the TMS-3, Flashlight, and Floodlight during the Turbosound era, and the Resolution, Evolution, and Vero systems at Funktion-One.

## Design Philosophy

**Tags:** `front_loaded_horn`, `constant_q_horn`, `point_source_coherent`, `low_distortion`, `acoustic_flat`

Funktion-One's philosophy centers on three core principles:

1. **Acoustic Flatness:** Rather than relying on corrective EQ, they design "acoustically flat loudspeaker systems, requiring only crossover filters, relative delays and gains." This preserves headroom and system linearity.

2. **Efficiency Focus:** Maximizes loudspeaker efficiency to reduce power consumption while delivering dynamic sound. Significantly fewer cabinets and amplifiers required vs. competitors.

3. **Custom Engineering:** Designs own transducers with unique waveguide and loading technology for minimum distortion and controlled directivity.

## Core Technologies

### 1. Turbo Mid-Range Loading (US6650760B1)
- **Patent:** US6650760B1, filed 2000, granted 2003
- **Inventors:** Anthony J Andrews, Toby C.C. Hunt, John Newsham
- **Assignee:** Funktion One
- **Principle:** Central member positioned inside horn creating annular acoustic channels with expanding and contracting regions. Achieves 107 dB @ 1W/1m sensitivity across 75-750 Hz.
- **Key Innovation:** The "constriction" in the second region lowers cutoff from ~150-200 Hz to ~75 Hz, enabling seamless bass-to-mid integration.

### 2. Neodymium Driver Design
- Funktion-One designs proprietary drivers with 6" voice coils and neodymium magnets
- 21" driver frame size pioneered by the company
- Paper cones preferred over metal for "organic" sound character

### 3. Lambda Rigging System
- 40 years of refinement of rear tension rigging methods
- Cabinets transport and fly as straight columns, tension into pre-set angles once suspended
- Optimized rotation axis ensures alignment at 0-12.5° for coherent summation

### 4. Axhead Loading
- Wide bandwidth cone midrange loading
- Single 8" or 10" driver covering 280Hz-5kHz (4+ octaves)
- Reduces crossover points and associated phase issues

## Technical Analysis & Criticisms

### Strengths (per design philosophy)
- **Efficiency:** 107dB @ 1W/1m achieved on mid-range
- **Transient Response:** Excellent fast impulse response with minimal, clean signals
- **Horn Loading:** Full horn loading throughout frequency range maintains headroom

### Criticisms (per independent analysis [7])
- **Midrange Resolution:** Single 10" driver across 250-4000 Hz may exhibit masking with complex, multi-layered material
- **Doppler Distortion:** Intermodulation when cone vibrating at one frequency handles another simultaneously
- **Coverage Uniformity:** Point-source creates uneven SPL (front vs. rear) vs. line arrays
- **Software Tools:** Lacks integrated prediction software; relies on manual EASE modeling
