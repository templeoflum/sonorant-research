## Company Overview

KV2 Audio was founded in 2002 by George Krampera and Marcelo Vercelli (the "K" and "V" in KV2, with "2" representing their second company). The first products premiered at NAMM in January 2003. Vercelli departed in 2005, and George Krampera Jr. (son) became CEO. The company remains fully family-owned and operates from a 100,000 sq ft facility in Czech Republic with one of the world's largest anechoic chambers.

## Design Philosophy

**Tags:** `point_source_coherent`, `low_distortion`, `transient_response`, `trans_coil`, `time_alignment`

KV2's philosophy centers on three critical elements: **frequency, level, and time**. They emphasize that most audio professionals focus on frequency response specs without understanding how all three dimensions work together.

Core principles:
1. **Point Source Over Line Array:** "Minimal destructive interference in higher frequency range, accurate impulse response and superior sound"
2. **Distortion Elimination:** "Removal of unwanted resonances" from transducer mechanical design
3. **Signal Integrity:** Maintain signal path integrity from source to output
4. **Purpose-Built Amplification:** Design amplifiers from ground up for specific applications, not generic solutions

### Notable Quotes

> "The true reproduction of the original sound and its dynamics are the key elements in the development of our products."
> — KV2 Audio [1]

> "The challenge for KV2 in building a large point source system for audiences of 50,000 people plus, was to reproduce the low mid energy that certain line arrays provide whilst maintaining the key benefits of point source technology."
> — George Krampera [2]

## Core Technologies

### 1. Trans-Coil Technology
- **Principle:** Woofer has two coils — standard voice coil plus second coil on neodymium pole piece
- **Benefit:** Eliminates voice coil inductance, resulting in flat impedance above resonance and faster transient response through increased force and control

### 2. NVPD Titanium Compression Drivers
- **Technology:** Nitrate Vapour Particle Deposition titanium
- **Configuration:** 3-diaphragm drivers with complex geometry phase plugs
- **Implementation:** Custom manifold horn assemblies with summing waveguides

### 3. Twin Asymmetrical Chamber Loading
- **Application:** ES and EX series subwoofers
- **Benefit:** Front-loaded configuration with large-format porting for improved transient response

### 4. Constant Power Point Source Array (VHD5)
- **Principle:** Modular point source system with minimal destructive interference
- **Scale:** Designed for 50,000+ audiences while maintaining point source benefits

## Case Studies

### Egg LDN (London, UK)

- **Type:** Warehouse-style nightclub + corporate events
- **Capacity:** ~1,000+ (multi-room)
- **System (Middle Floor):**
  - 4× SL4.12 mid/hi (110° H dispersion)
  - 4× VHD4.21 subs (14,000W peak each)
  - 2× ES2.6 (2×15")
  - DJ monitors: EX15 + ES1.8
- **Outcomes:**
  - "Super low distortion levels"

## Technical Observations

### Strengths (Per Design Claims)
- **Transient Response:** Trans-Coil technology specifically addresses voice coil inductance
- **Point Source Coherence:** Single apparent source eliminates timing/phase issues
- **Low Distortion:** Multiple mechanisms including phase-aligned horn assemblies

### User Feedback
- Generally praised for definition and performance vs. price
- Some note HF horn is "a little lifted" — typically roll back 1-2 dB
- Favorably compared to HK Actor, RCF ART, JBL SR, Nexo PS10, APG DS

- **No Patents Found:** Search did not identify granted patents; "patent-pending" claims only
